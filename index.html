❓ Les adjectifs interrogatifs — Quel / Quelle
📖 What are Interrogative Adjectives?

Used to ask "Which?" or "What?" before a noun.
They change based on gender (Masculine/Feminine) and number (Singular/Plural).
Note: Quel/Quels both sound like "Kel" · Quelle/Quelles both sound like "Kell"

👦 Masculine Singular — Kel
Quel

Quel livre ? kel leevr — Which book?
Quel garçon ? kel gar-son — Which boy?
Quel étudiant ? kel ay-tood-yan — Which student?

👦👦 Masculine Plural — Kel
Quels

Quels livres ? kel leevr — Which books?
Quels garçons ? kel gar-son — Which boys?
Quels étudiants ? kel ay-tood-yan — Which students?

👧 Feminine Singular — Kell
Quelle

Quelle voiture ? kell vwa-toor — Which car?
Quelle ville ? kell veel — Which city?
Quelle école ? kell ay-kol — Which school?

👧👧 Feminine Plural — Kell
Quelles

Quelles voitures ? kell vwa-toor — Which cars?
Quelles villes ? kell veel — Which cities?
Quelles écoles ? kell ay-kol — Which schools?

🧠 Memory Trick
—ONE 👤MANY 👥
👦 MasculineQuelQuels
👧 FeminineQuelleQuelles

Quel → Boy + One 👦  ·  Quelle → Girl + One 👧
Quels → Boys + Many 👦👦  ·  Quelles → Girls + Many 👧👧

👤 Les Pronoms
Subject pronouns and tonic (stressed) pronouns — full recap
📖 Les pronoms sujets — Subject Pronouns

The pronoun that performs the action of the verb — placed right before the conjugated verb.

PersonSingularPlural
1stJe (I)Nous (we)
2ndTu (you, informal)Vous (you, formal/plural)
3rdIl (he/it, mas.) · Elle (she/it, fem.)Ils (they, mas.) · Elles (they, fem.)
📖 Les pronoms toniques — Tonic (Stressed) Pronouns

Used for emphasis, after prepositions, or alone — never directly before a verb as the subject.

PersonSingularPlural
1stMoi (me/myself)Nous (us/ourselves)
2ndToi (you/yourself)Vous (you/yourselves)
3rdLui (him/himself) · Elle (her/herself)Eux (them, mas.) · Elles (them, fem.)
📰 Les Articles
Indéfinis (a/an/some) and Définis (the)
📖 i) Les articles indéfinis — un / une / des

Means a / an / some in English.

un — masculine
un garçon, un livre, un stylo, un chat, un bus
une — feminine
une fille, une voiture, une chatte, une pomme
des — plural (mas/fem)
des garçons, des livres, des filles, des pommes
🧠 When to use it

• To introduce an object  ·  To indicate a random fact  ·  Multiple existence

Il y a un restaurant dans la rue Balzac.  ·  J'ai une amie.  ·  C'est un livre.

📖 ii) Les articles définis — le / la / l' / les

Means "the" in English.

le — masculine
le garçon, le livre, le chat, le stylo, le bus
la — feminine
la voiture, la fille, la table, la voisine
l' — mas/fem, before a vowel
l'ami, l'amie, l'hôtel, l'école
les — any plural
les livres, les voitures, les hôtels
🧠 When to use it

• To indicate solid facts  ·  To indicate specific/established objects  ·  From the second mention of an object onward

C'est le livre de Bhargavi.  ·  La Tour Eiffel se trouve à Paris.  ·  Le Panda est un animal habitant en Chine.

🧠 Easy Rule

Masculine noun → mon, ton, son — Mon livre (my book) · Ton frère (your brother) · Son père (his/her father)

⭐ Important Rule

In French, possessive adjectives agree with the noun, NOT with the owner. They agree with gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular/plural) — never with the person who owns the thing.

This is my car. Why ma? Because voiture = feminine noun.

I speak to my mother. mère = feminine → use ma.

You are going to France with your uncle. oncle = masculine → use ton.

❓ Doubt / Viva Questions
Common student questions about possessive adjectives
Do possessive adjectives agree with the owner?
❌ No. They agree with the noun (gender and number), not with the owner.
Why do we say Mon école instead of Ma école?
Because école starts with a vowel (é). French avoids "Ma école" — it changes to "Mon école" for easier pronunciation.
Is Mon only for masculine nouns?
No — it's also used for feminine nouns beginning with a vowel or silent h. E.g. Mon père, Mon école, Mon amie.
Does Son mean only "his"?
No. Son can mean "his" or "her" — French doesn't distinguish here. Context tells you whose it is.
Why are Notre and Votre the same for masculine and feminine?
Because Notre and Votre do not change for singular nouns, regardless of gender.
What is the difference between Leur and Leurs?
Leur → one thing (singular noun). Leurs → many things (plural noun). E.g. Leur maison / Leurs maisons.
Can Mon be used before all feminine nouns?
❌ No — only feminine nouns starting with a vowel or silent h. Mon voiture and Mon mère are both wrong.
Is Notre singular or plural?
It depends on the possessed noun, not the owner. Notre maison = one house; Nos maisons = many houses — even though Nous (we) is always plural.
Is Votre used only for many people?
No — Votre is used for one person (formal) or many people. E.g. Votre nom, Madame? / Votre classe.

❓ Why "Je vais en France" but "Je vais au Japon"? — France is feminine → en. Japon is masculine → au.

❓ When do we use en and à for transport? — en = closed transport (bus, train, taxi, airplane). à = open transport or movement (à pied, à vélo, à cheval).

🧠 Final Revision — Coming FROM / Going TO

Feminine: de/d' (from) · en (to)  ·  Masculine: du (from) · au (to)  ·  Plural: des (from) · aux (to)  ·  City: de (from) · à (to)

👤 Le Pronom Sujet "On"
An important beginner topic
📘 What is "On"?

On is a subject pronoun. Usually it means We (in spoken French), and sometimes One / People / Someone (general meaning).

👉 Simple rule: "On" = "Nous" (We) in everyday conversation.

⭐ Important Rule

Although On means "We", the verb is always conjugated like "Il/Elle" (3rd person singular).

Formal (Nous)Spoken (On)English
Nous parlonsOn parleWe speak
Nous allonsOn vaWe go
Nous mangeonsOn mangeWe eat
Nous écrivonsOn écritWe write
Nous avonsOn aWe have
Nous sommesOn estWe are
Nous prenonsOn prendWe take
🇫🇷 On va à l'école. On Va Ah Lay-kol
We are going to school.
🇫🇷 On regarde la télévision. On Ruh-gard Lah Tay-lay-vee-zee-on
We are watching television.
🇫🇷 On achète les billets. On Ah-shet Lay Bee-yay
We buy the tickets.
🎯 Memory Trick

👉 Meaning = "We" (Nous)  ·  👉 Verb = "Il/Elle" form

"On" means "we", but it always takes the same verb form as "il/elle" (3rd person singular). This is one of the most common patterns in everyday spoken French.

📘 Danser (To Dance) — full conjugation showing the On pattern

Je danse · Tu danses · Il/Elle/On danse · Nous dansons · Vous dansez · Ils/Elles dansent

Note: Je danse, Tu danses, Il danse, On danse and Ils dansent all sound identical when spoken (the -es and -ent endings are silent). Only Nous dansons and Vous dansez sound different.

🧠 Student Doubts

❓ Is On the same as Nous? — Almost. Both mean "We", but Nous is formal/written French, while On is everyday spoken French.

❓ Why is it On est and not On sommes? — Because On always uses the Il/Elle verb form: Il est, Elle est, On est ✅
